Why Greens

The most valuable
surface on the course.

And the most expensive thing on a golf course to lose. A single hydraulic event on a green can mean seasonal closure, soil remediation, resodding, and the kind of conversation with ownership that ends careers.

A green is not a fairway.

Fairways are forgiving. They’re wide, lower-cut, and built to recover. A hydraulic stripe across a fairway is a six-week problem, an awkward conversation, a budget line you’d rather not explain.

Greens are not forgiving. They are the most carefully maintained surface on any golf course — ultra-low cut, drainage-engineered, surfaced with putting-grade bentgrass or bermuda that takes years to mature. Oil contamination doesn’t just kill the grass. It poisons the rootzone.

Once oil hits a green, you’re no longer talking about recovery. You’re talking about remediation. Excavation. Resod. And until the new turf is established, the green is closed.
