About Iris

The eye that sees
what operators can’t.

Iris is a technology company building precision sensing and machine intelligence for golf course turf management. The name is deliberate — the iris is the part of the eye that controls vision and perception. We are the sensory layer between turf equipment and the people responsible for the course.

Mission

Precision sensing for golf course turf.

Golf course management lives at the intersection of art, science, and constant vigilance. The superintendent is responsible for the most carefully maintained outdoor surface on Earth — and held accountable for damage they can’t always anticipate.

Iris exists to close that gap. We build precision instruments that watch what operators can’t, surface data the eye can’t see, and provide the kind of always-on awareness that human attention alone can never deliver.

We start with the most catastrophic, most preventable failure mode in turf operations: hydraulic oil on grass. TurfVision is the first answer. It will not be the last.

How we work

Built in the field.

Iris products are designed by people who understand turf operations — not by a tech company trying to disrupt agronomy from a distance. Every detail is tested in the conditions superintendents actually face.

Technology

The instrument behind the eye.

TurfVision is built on a patent-pending long-wave infrared detection algorithm, engineered specifically for the thermal differential between hydraulic fluid and turf canopy. The underlying technology is developed by a team with deep experience in machine autonomy, sensor fusion, and field-deployed robotics.

The same engineering foundation extends into the next products in the Iris arc — agronomic intelligence and, ultimately, autonomous turf operations.
