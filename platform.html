The Iris Platform

TurfVision is the first eye.
Not the last.

Iris is building the sensory and intelligence layer for the next generation of golf course operations. TurfVision is product one in a deliberate three-product arc — each one building infrastructure for the next.

The Arc

Three products. One platform.

Hardware first. Data next. Autonomy after. Customers don’t need to commit to anything beyond TurfVision today — but everything that follows runs on the same chassis.

Product 01 · Available Now

TurfVision

Real-time IR hydraulic leak detection. Alert tier and optional Shutdown tier. Installs on any hydraulic-equipped turf machine.

What it builds: Brand trust. Installed hardware base. Direct superintendent relationships.

Product 02 · Year 2 In development

Iris Agronomic Intelligence

While TurfVision is scanning for leaks, it’s also collecting infrared imagery of the entire turf surface. A subscription unlocks a daily heat map of the property — turf stress, dry zones, irrigation inconsistencies, thermal anomalies invisible to the naked eye.

Superintendents see the course in a way they never have before.

Expected pricing: $299–$499 per course per year.
What it builds: Recurring revenue. Daily workflow integration. The data platform.

Product 03 · Horizon Under R&D

Autonomous Fleet Operations

The next generation of turf equipment doesn’t need an operator. The machines drive themselves. They mow on schedule, navigate around play, and return to the shop when they’re done.

By the time the autonomous system reaches market, Iris customers already have the sensors mounted, the data flowing, and the trust earned. The upgrade is incremental, not revolutionary.

What it builds: The future of golf course operations.

Why this matters now

Today’s sensor is tomorrow’s autonomy.

The hardware mounted on your fairway mower this season is the same hardware that will guide the autonomous mower three seasons from now. The mapping data TurfVision collects today is the foundation of the agronomic intelligence dashboard next year — and the navigation layer after that.

Choosing TurfVision isn’t a maintenance decision. It’s a position in a long arc that ends with golf courses operating more efficiently, more sustainably, and with fewer surprises than any other point in the industry’s history.
